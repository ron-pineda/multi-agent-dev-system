Agent Team — Usage Guide

You're the founder. You talk to the PM. The PM runs the 28-agent team.

Your Role

You don't manage agents directly. Your job:

  • Share ideas and goals with PM — PM translates them into work
  • Approve proposed tasks in the dashboard — nothing moves without your approval
  • Check the INBOX — agents flag items that genuinely need your decision
  • Provide direction when blocked — if a task is stuck, the answer usually lives with you

You don't write briefs, assign engineers, or track handoffs. PM and the rest of the team handle all of that.

How Work Flows

The core build pipeline — every feature goes through this:

You→ PM→ Research (if needed)→ Designer (if UI)→ Architect→ Engineers→ QA→ Reviewer→ Done
  1. You tell PM what you want to build
  2. If the idea needs investigation, PM hands to Research first
  3. For UI-heavy features, PM routes to Designer for a design spec before planning
  4. PM writes a brief and asks Architect to plan it
  5. Architect breaks into tasks with acceptance criteria and assigns the right engineers
  6. You approve proposed tasks in the dashboard
  7. Engineers build — Frontend, Backend, Database, Mobile, AI Engineer, or Integrations as needed
  8. QA verifies each task against acceptance criteria
  9. Reviewer checks legibility, safety, and standards — marks done
  10. Doc Writer updates README, CHANGELOG, and comments

Specialist agents (Security, Performance, Analytics, Growth, etc.) are invoked on demand — not in every pipeline run.

Build — core engineering

PM

Sonnet / Haiku

Your primary interface. Asks clarifying questions, writes briefs, gates tasks, runs weekly reviews, handles STOP. Every interaction starts here.

Needs you for: Kickoff questions, direction changes, INBOX items.

Architect

Opus / Sonnet

Breaks features into discrete, independently-buildable tasks. Writes acceptance criteria. Assigns engineers. Records architecture decisions.

Needs you for: Rarely — surfaces conflicts to PM who surfaces to you.

Frontend Engineer

Sonnet

Builds UI: screens, components, interactions, styling. Self-checks before handing to Reviewer.

Needs you for: Almost never.

Backend Engineer

Sonnet

APIs, business logic, auth, server-side features. Goes to QA before Reviewer.

Needs you for: Missing credentials or blocked integrations.

Database Engineer

Sonnet

Schema changes, migrations, queries, indexes. Never runs production migrations without explicit approval.

Needs you for: Production migration runs.

Mobile

Sonnet

Flutter/Dart, Android/iOS native features. Handles app store build artifacts — hands deployments to DevOps.

Needs you for: App store credentials, provisioning profiles.

AI Engineer

Sonnet / Opus

System prompt design, model selection, eval harnesses. Never changes production prompts without running evals.

Needs you for: Approving prompt changes in production.

Designer

Sonnet

Translates vision into buildable design specs. Every output includes exact copy — no placeholders. Feeds directly into Architect planning.

Needs you for: Visual direction and brand alignment questions.

Integrations

Sonnet

Third-party API integrations, webhooks, OAuth flows. Always reads official docs. Webhook signature verification is mandatory.

Needs you for: API credentials and third-party account access.

Quality — correctness & safety

QA

Haiku / Sonnet

Verifies every task against acceptance criteria. Binary pass/fail — no grey zone. Routes to Reviewer or back to Architect with specific reasons.

Needs you for: Never directly.

Reviewer

Sonnet

Final gate: code legibility, safety, standards. Approves → done, or requests rework with specific feedback.

Needs you for: Never directly.

Doc Writer

Haiku

README updates, inline comments, CHANGELOG entries. Only documents what actually exists.

Needs you for: Never directly.

Security

Sonnet

Threat modeling, OWASP audits, RLS reviews. Produces severity-rated reports. Never modifies code — findings become Architect tasks.

Needs you for: Deciding which severity findings to prioritize.

Performance

Haiku / Sonnet

Profiling, bottleneck analysis, optimization plans. Baseline-first discipline — never recommends optimization without measuring.

Needs you for: Rarely — findings route through Architect.

Operations — keeping the lights on

DevOps

Sonnet

CI/CD pipelines, deployments, environment management, app store submissions. Never deploys to production without explicit human sign-off.

Needs you for: Every production deployment.

SRE

Sonnet

Monitoring setup, incident response, runbooks, post-mortems. On-call escalation paths. Never touches production without instruction.

Needs you for: Incident calls, production changes.

Analytics

Sonnet

Event tracking schema, usage reports, funnel analysis. Server-side events preferred. Read-only on production data.

Needs you for: PII tracking decisions.

Finance

Haiku / Sonnet

Unit economics, API cost modeling, revenue projections. Every estimate shows three scenarios (optimistic, base, conservative). Assumptions are explicit.

Needs you for: Actual revenue/cost numbers to work with.

Legal

Sonnet

Privacy policy, ToS, GDPR/CCPA, app store compliance checklists. Always flags when the situation needs real legal counsel.

Needs you for: Final sign-off before publishing policies.

Chief of Staff

Sonnet

Weekly portfolio briefing across all projects: what shipped, what's stuck, what decisions are pending. Ask when you want the full picture fast.

Needs you for: Access to answer "Decisions Needed" items it surfaces.

Growth — finding users

Growth

Haiku / Sonnet

App Store Optimization, SEO audits, launch strategy. Data-driven — every recommendation has a measurable outcome and a next action.

Needs you for: Approving launch plans before anything ships.

Brand

Sonnet

Brand guide (positioning, personality, voice, colors, typography). Consistency review flags drift. Run once early; update when direction changes.

Needs you for: Core positioning and personality decisions.

Copywriter

Haiku / Sonnet

Every word users read: landing pages, onboarding, CTAs, error messages, app store listings, email sequences. Shorter is almost always better.

Needs you for: Copy approval before shipping.

Content

Haiku / Sonnet

Content strategy, build-in-public threads, Product Hunt launches, newsletters. Consistency over bursts. Never publishes without explicit approval.

Needs you for: Approving every piece before it goes out.

Customer Success

Sonnet

Feedback triage (Bug / Feature / Confusion / Positive), pilot health checks, support templates. Surfaces patterns — never promises features or timelines.

Needs you for: Acting on escalated feedback and pilot check-in calls.

Business — strategy & revenue

Strategist

Sonnet

Pricing analysis, competitive positioning (one-sentence output), GTM planning. Every output includes "What This Assumes" — so you know what it's built on.

Needs you for: Final decisions on pricing and go-to-market direction.

Research

Sonnet

Pre-brief investigation: technical feasibility, market landscape, competitor analysis, build-vs-buy. Hands findings back to PM — never makes the final call.

Needs you for: The decision after the research is in.

Sales

Sonnet

Prospect research, cold outreach drafts (5-sentence max), pitch materials, 3-touch follow-up sequences. Never contacts anyone without per-message human approval.

Needs you for: Approving every outreach message before it sends.

Common Scenarios

"I have a new feature idea"

Tell PM. PM may route to Research first if the idea needs investigation, then to Designer if it's UI-heavy, then to Architect to plan it. You'll see proposed tasks in the dashboard to approve.

"I want to know if my idea is viable before building"

Tell PM to run a Research sprint first. Research produces a findings doc in docs/research/ with competitive landscape, technical feasibility, and risks. PM then decides whether to proceed.

"I want to launch on Product Hunt / grow the user base"

Tell PM. PM routes to Growth for ASO/SEO/launch planning, Copywriter for copy, and Content for the post-launch content calendar. All plans come back to you for approval before anything ships.

"I need to understand our pricing / unit economics"

Ask PM to run a Finance + Strategist analysis. Finance handles the numbers (unit economics, cost model, revenue scenarios), Strategist handles the positioning and GTM implications.

"Something looks wrong / I want to stop work"

Click STOP on the project card. This abandons all in-progress tasks and asks PM for new direction.

"I want to change direction mid-build"

Tell PM. PM will invoke STOP on the relevant tasks, then re-brief Architect with the new direction.

"A task has been sitting there for days"

Check the task — it may be blocked. Look at the blockers field. The answer is usually a decision or credential only you can provide. Leave a comment or respond to the INBOX item.

"I want to see what everyone is working on"

Go to the Team tab — shows all 28 agents with current assignments. Go to the Activity tab for the full chronological handoff log.

"I want a weekly status across all projects"

Ask Chief of Staff for the weekly briefing. It reads all projects simultaneously and delivers: what shipped, what's stuck, what decisions are pending, and what to focus on.
